How distribution works

The faucet drips  21,000,000 Krai Per Hour  spread across any participating accounts for the prior 2-hour period. Each one of your successes increases your overall success count for the current undistributed period. When our algorithm calculates distribution percentages by account, it considers the total success count for that account against the overall success count of everyone for that period.


Distribution Algorithm

Krai = ( My Count / Total Count ) * ( 21,000,000 * Hours Since Last Distributed )

Distribution Method

Our system finalizes total calculations for the last 2-hour period prior to actually distributing Krai. This process occurs every 2 hours. In the event that distribution takes longer than normal, please don't worry! All of your successful ReCaptcha solves are saved forever in our system and it's very clear which accounts have not beed paid making it easy for us to retroactively pay. We are working towards preventing all hiccups in our automated distribution process though and it won't be an issue soon.

The realtime data shown in the top row of 'My Past Distributions' shows your current progress in the unpaid distribution period. It shows you a projection that's likely to change after finalization calculations occur. It should be a fairly accurate representation of what you will get receive in distribution, especially if you are highly-active near the 2-hour mark.


Some advice

Keep in mind that any requests faster than 6 seconds from your last successful request will fail. It's difficult to be this fast, but it's possible in certain cases. In that case, you would receive a Try Again message, so be careful not to be too quick!

There are also currently rare cases where your distribution request will get stuck and you will keep getting a Try Again warning. This should resolve itself within 1 minute though, so please be patient if that does happen.
