Giant Pandas

Solitary Munchers

The 'giant panda', also known as 'panda bear', or simply 'panda', is a native bear to south-central China. With easily spotted features such as black eye patches and their white backs & bellies, these bears are quite a unique creature.

Although they're classified as a carnivore, this fuzzy tree-crawler tends to graze on bamboo, fully living up to it's stereotype. Wild Giant pandas will occasionally eat other grasses, wild tubers, or even meat in the form of birds, rodents or carrion. While captive, pandas can receive honey, eggs, fish, yams, shrub leaves, oranges, or bananas along with specially prepared food.
